What is a GPU Stack? A Simple Guide

To understand our business, it helps to know what we're building. Think of it like an orchestra. A regular computer is like a single, talented musician. But to solve the biggest problems, you need a full orchestra of specialists working together. That's what a GPU data center is.

1. The GPU: The Specialist Musician

A GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) is a special kind of computer chip. Originally made for video games, they are amazing at doing thousands of simple math problems all at once. This ability, called **parallel processing**, is exactly what's needed for Artificial Intelligence. Each GPU is like a brilliant specialist musician.

2. The Server: The Orchestra Section

You group musicians into sections (like violins or cellos). A **server** is a powerful computer that houses a group of GPUs, typically 8 of them. It provides power and a high-speed connection to data. Because these GPUs get very hot, our servers use a special **water-cooling system**‚Äîlike a high-tech plumbing system‚Äîto keep them running perfectly.

3. The Rack: The Stage

Orchestra sections perform on a stage. A **rack** is a tall metal frame that holds many servers stacked vertically. This allows us to pack an enormous amount of computing power into a small physical space, all connected with networking, power, and the main pipes for our water-cooling system.

4. The Cluster: The Full Orchestra

Finally, you bring all the sections together to form the full orchestra. A **GPU cluster** is a room filled with many racks, all interconnected with ultra-fast networking. This allows thousands of GPUs to work together on a single, massive problem. **Our business is building these "orchestras" in Nepal and renting out their combined power.**
